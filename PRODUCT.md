# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single, motivated individual (Islom) and, later, friends he invites — each with
their own isolated account. They are students / makers / creators in their late
teens to twenties working toward self-directed goals (language exams, fitness,
personal brand, side projects). Context of use: a private daily ritual — opening
the dashboard in the morning or evening to orient the day, log reflection, and
run focus blocks. Not a team tool; there is one user per screen, all data
private via row-level security.

## Product Purpose

A personal "life operating system" — a calm second brain for goals, projects,
ideas, daily journaling, focus sessions, and progress. It exists to make
long-term effort feel visible and emotionally rewarding, so the user keeps
showing up. Success = the user *wants* to open it daily and feels "I am managing
my life from one beautiful place."

## Brand Personality

Calm, premium, inspiring, intentional. Three words: **serene, focused,
aspirational.** Voice is quiet and encouraging, never loud or nagging. The feel
is Apple × Linear × Notion × Arc — restraint, generous space, soft motion. It
should feel like a private studio at night, not a control panel.

## Anti-references

- **Colorful gamified apps** (Duolingo / Habitica): bright primaries, badges,
  confetti, childish reward loops. Avoid.
- **Dense productivity tools** (Jira / ClickUp): toolbar overload, packed panels,
  cognitive overwhelm. Avoid.
- **Generic AI-template look**: the same glass + gradient + emoji scaffold
  everywhere that reads as "an AI made this." Avoid; earn every effect.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over busy.** Default to space and silence; reveal depth on intent. One
   clear focus per screen.
2. **Effort made visible.** Progress, streaks, and momentum are the emotional
   payload — show the climb, not vanity metrics.
3. **Earn every effect.** Glass, blur, and glow are used purposefully and
   sparingly, never as decoration-by-reflex.
4. **Motion with meaning.** Animation guides attention and rewards action; it is
   subtle, exponential-eased, and always has a reduced-motion fallback.
5. **Personal, not corporate.** It greets you by name and adapts to your life;
   it should feel owned, intimate, and quiet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1 against the near-black
surfaces. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere (crossfade / instant
fallback for every transition). Dark mode only is an intentional product
decision, not an a11y gap; ensure muted-gray text stays above contrast floors.
