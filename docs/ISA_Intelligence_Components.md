# ISA — Intelligence Components

> **Status:** Official · Foundational  
> **Companion to:** All five foundational documents (Core Philosophy, Life Intelligence Engine, AI Behavior Rules, Premium Blueprint, Product Roadmap)  
> **Purpose:** Define the conceptual intelligence components of ISA at the level of responsibility and behavior — never implementation details.

These components exist **inside** the Life Intelligence Engine. They are not separate products or technical modules to be coded independently. They are responsibilities the Engine must fulfill.

--- 

## Core Principle

Every intelligence component must obey the permanent laws from the five foundational documents:

- Identity outranks optimization
- Human agency is sacred (human decides, ISA advises)
- Silence is the default
- Understanding over information
- Calm over engagement
- Teach instead of create dependency
- Honesty and explainability always

--- 

## 1. AI Memory

**Conceptual Role:**  
The part of the Engine responsible for what ISA actively remembers and what it deliberately forgets about a person’s life.

**Must do:**
- Remember meaningful patterns, declared identity, values, and pivotal life events.
- Maintain stable baselines that help interpret new events.
- Support long-term relationship evolution (from Day 1 organizer to Year 10 wise companion).

**Must never:**
- Remember trivial one-off events as if they define the person.
- Trap the user in outdated versions of themselves.
- Treat temporary behavior as permanent character.
- Store data in a way that feels like surveillance.

**Alignment with foundational documents:**
- Directly supports “Memory Behavior” from AI Behavior Rules.
- Serves the “Teach instead of Store” principle from Core Philosophy.
- Enables the long-term relationship model in the Product Roadmap.

--- 

## 2. AI Context Engine

**Conceptual Role:**  
The part of the Engine that views every event through multiple lenses (current moment, today, week, month, year, lifetime) and always weighs it against the user’s declared Identity Layer.

**Must do:**
- Maintain multiple context layers simultaneously.
- Give the highest priority to Identity (who the user wants to become).
- Fuse Identity + Life Context + Behavior Context + Current Moment into a Decision Context before any recommendation or insight.

**Must never:**
- Treat events in isolation (silo thinking).
- Ignore the user’s stated values or aspirational identity.
- Create context that pressures the user.

**Alignment:**
- Core of the Life Intelligence Engine’s Layer 4 (Context + Identity).
- Directly enforces “Identity outranks optimization” from Core Philosophy.
- Enables the calm, relevant, identity-aligned intelligence described in AI Behavior Rules and Premium Blueprint.

--- 

## 3. Life Database (Conceptual)

**Conceptual Role:**  
The single source of truth for all meaningful life events. It is not a raw storage system — it is a structured, provenance-bearing record of a person’s life.

**Must do:**
- Capture every meaningful action as a normalized Life Event with clear provenance.
- Preserve an honest, append-only history.
- Support the graph of relationships between events across domains.

**Must never:**
- Become a dumping ground for every click or minor action.
- Allow editing of historical truth (corrections create new events).
- Treat absence of data as failure.

**Alignment:**
- Foundation of the entire Life Intelligence Engine.
- Enables “Everything Connects” and “Intelligence over Information” from Core Philosophy.
- Provides the raw material for all higher intelligence components.

--- 

## 4. Prompt System (Conceptual)

**Conceptual Role:**  
The part of the Engine responsible for deciding **what** intelligence should be generated and **when** it should surface to the user. It is the guardian of attention.

**Must do:**
- Enforce a high threshold before any proactive message or insight appears.
- Arbitrate between many possible true things and select only the most valuable one (or none).
- Respect the user’s current capacity, context, and attention budget.
- Learn from user feedback (accepted vs dismissed) to improve future decisions.

**Must never:**
- Generate noise or low-value interruptions.
- Create addictive or pressure-based nudges.
- Act without strong justification from Identity, importance, and timeliness.

**Alignment:**
- Directly implements the Decision Engine from Life Intelligence Engine.
- Enforces “When ISA Should Speak” and “Human Agency Rules” from AI Behavior Rules.
- Protects the “Calm” and “Respect attention” principles from Premium Blueprint.

--- 

## 5. Analytics Engine

**Conceptual Role:**  
The part of the Engine that turns raw events into honest, explainable patterns and meaning.

**Must do:**
- Detect real, repeated, cross-domain patterns only when there is sufficient evidence.
- Attach clear reasoning to every insight (“because…”).
- Distinguish between fact, pattern, assumption, and recommendation.
- Support both reflection (what happened) and foresight (what is likely to happen).

**Must never:**
- Fabricate patterns from noise or thin data.
- Present correlation as causation.
- Create “black box” conclusions the user cannot inspect.

**Alignment:**
- Core of Layers 5–6 in the Life Intelligence Engine.
- Directly supports “Honesty Over Illusion” and “Reasoning Transparency” from the foundational documents.
- Enables the “understanding” that Premium Blueprint and Product Roadmap require.

--- 

## 6. Recommendation Engine

**Conceptual Role:**  
The part of the Engine that proposes optional next steps or actions, always grounded in the user’s own data and identity.

**Must do:**
- Generate recommendations that are:
  - Personalized to this specific person
  - Identity-aligned (rejected if they contradict who the user wants to become)
  - Explainable
  - Optional and reversible
  - Bounded by the user’s current capacity and values
- Present recommendations as proposals, never commands.

**Must never:**
- Command, shame, or manipulate.
- Optimize for productivity at the cost of well-being or values.
- Pretend certainty.

**Alignment:**
- Directly implements “How ISA Gives Advice” from AI Behavior Rules.
- Serves the “Human Agency Rules” and “Teach instead of create dependency” principles.
- Supports the calm, respectful experience defined in Premium Blueprint.

--- 

## 7. Prediction Engine

**Conceptual Role:**  
The part of the Engine responsible for thoughtful foresight — helping the user see what is likely to happen so they can act early.

**Must do:**
- Produce probabilistic, explainable forecasts only when data supports them.
- Focus on prevention and preparation rather than alarm.
- Always include the reasoning and confidence level.
- Only surface predictions that are actionable and respect attention.

**Must never:**
- Make dramatic or fear-based predictions.
- Predict things the user cannot influence.
- Present predictions as certainties.
- Use predictions to create pressure or engagement.

**Alignment:**
- Supports Stage 2 and Stage 3 of the Product Roadmap (Personal Intelligence Layer → Life Operating System).
- Enforces “Honesty” and “Calm” from all foundational documents.
- Only activates meaningfully after sufficient history exists (respects long-term relationship model).

---

## Overall Rules for All Components

1. Every component is subordinate to the **Identity Layer** and **Human Agency**.
2. Every component must be able to output **nothing** when that is the right answer.
3. Every output must be explainable and traceable back to real events.
4. No component may create dependency or reduce the user’s own capability over time.
5. All components together must make the user feel: “ISA understands me and helps me improve my life — calmly and respectfully.”

---

**End of ISA — Intelligence Components**

These seven conceptual components together form the intelligence heart of the Life Intelligence Engine. They are defined by responsibility and behavior, not by technology. Any future technical implementation must honor these definitions exactly.
